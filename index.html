Source of Data: The data of London's 1854 Cholera outbreak was created and compiled by Robin Wilson based on John Snow's Cholera analysis.

The chart above is a representation of the data created by Robin Wilson based upon John Snow's Cholera analysis. The blue line represents the number of Cholera attacks that happened on each day. The orange line represents the number of Cholera deaths that happened on each day. The green line represents the total number of Cholera attacks that had happened up until that day. Lastly, the red line represents the total number of Cholera deaths that had happened up until that day. From this chart, we can tell that there was a huge spike of attacks near the end of Aug / beginning of Sep that caused the spike in deaths. This period was the major focal point of the outbreak.

The chart above is a representation of the number of male and female fatalities per 10,000 people of each age group. This chart plots age ranges on the x-axis and plots number of fatalities per 10,000 people of each gender on the y-axis. The blue bar represents the number of females per 10,000 people in each age group that died due to Cholera. The orange bar represents the number of males per 10,000 people in each age group that died due to Cholera. From the chart, we can infer that females and males have around the same number of fatalities, and elders were affected the most, followed by younger children.

The charts above are a representation of the 1851 UK Census. The pie chart plots the percentage of each age group that was recorded by the UK Census in 1851 and also shows the number of people per age group. The left chart represents all the males while the right chart represents all the females. The charts show that more of the population are on the younger side and the female & male age group ratios are similar.

The chart above are a representation of the 1851 UK Census. The pie chart shows the number of men vs women based on the 1851 UK Census. We can see that there were slightly more females than males at the time.

The chart above is a representation of the number of males and females in the population according to the 1851 UK Census. The blue bar represents the number of females per age group taken by the Census and the orange bar represents the number of males per age group taken by the Census. This chart tells us that we generally have more females than males in most age groups aside from 0-9, which also backs up the pie chart.
